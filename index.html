Provenance & cross-check. Parameter list, eigenvalues, existence status and known counts are taken verbatim from Brouwer's tables (retrieved 2026-05-30) and re-rendered here; the original HTML is archived under data/ for audit. The #new (ours) column counts graphs constructed in this project this session by an Ihringer-style Godsil–McKay switching cascade from low-symmetry de Caen seeds, each verified independently as a valid SRG (degree, λ, μ) with automorphism order computed by dreadnaut. These parameters are Brouwer-“+” (exist, not fully classified); our graphs are asymmetric (|Aut| not divisible by 6), hence outside every published prescribed-automorphism enumeration. Method and caveats: docs/new_srgs_2026_05_30.md. Second cross-check — SageMath. The Sage DB column is the verdict of sage.graphs.strongly_regular_graph(v,k,λ,μ, existence=True) (Cohen–Pasechnik's programmatic implementation of Brouwer's tables): “✓ builds” = Sage constructs a representative, “?” = existence unknown to Sage, “∄” = Sage proves non-existence. For our three parameters Sage independently confirms existence, and every sampled graph of ours was re-verified by Sage's is_strongly_regular() and found non-isomorphic to Sage's representative (see scripts/sage_srg_crosscheck.sage).
